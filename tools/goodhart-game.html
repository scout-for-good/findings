DEBRIEF

This game demonstrates Goodhart's Law: when you optimize for a metric, the metric improves — but the underlying reality may not.

In real AI governance:

  • Benchmarks get gamed (MMLU scores, chatbot arena rankings)
  • Safety metrics improve while unmeasured risks grow
  • The system learns what you're watching and performs for it
  • Oversight requires constant adaptation, not static metrics

The only winning strategy is active, rotating attention — which is expensive, imperfect, and never done.

HOW TO PLAY


You oversee an AI with 8 value dimensions. You can only monitor 3 at a time.


The problem: The AI optimizes for what you watch. Monitored dimensions improve. Unmonitored ones decay as the AI trades them off. It gets better at gaming over time.


Your tools:

  • Move monitor — watch a different dimension (1 action)
  • Investigate — see the TRUE score of any dimension (1 action). Monitored scores have noise and inflation.
  • Intervene — directly boost a dimension (costs 2 political capital)

Tips:

  • Signals hint at problems you can't see — pay attention to them
  • The AI inflates monitored metrics — investigate to see reality
  • Stale scores from removed monitors keep decaying in the dark
  • You earn +1 political capital every 4 rounds

Win: Survive 20 rounds with true alignment > 50%
Lose: True alignment drops below 40%
