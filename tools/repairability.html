Methodology & Sources

This tool synthesizes publicly available scoring criteria from two regulatory frameworks:

French Repairability Index (Indice de réparabilité, 2021): Five equally-weighted criteria scored out of 20 each, totaling 100, then divided by 10. Mandatory on smartphones, laptops, TVs, washing machines, lawnmowers, and more. Administered by France's Ministry of Ecological Transition.

EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR): A-E grading based on six weighted parameters covering disassembly depth, fastener types, tool requirements, spare parts, repair instructions, and software updates. In effect for smartphones and tablets since June 2025, expanding to more categories.

EN 45554:2020: European standard providing general methods for assessing repairability, reusability, and upgradability. This tool's parameter structure draws on its framework of disassemblability parameters (P1-P4) and process parameters (P5-P11).

Scores are estimates for design-phase decision-making, not formal compliance assessments. For regulatory compliance, use the official tools provided by your national authority. The French Ministry provides official calculation spreadsheets; the EU JRC maintains the ESPR product bureau documentation.

This tool is free and open-source. No data is collected or transmitted.
