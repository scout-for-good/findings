THE SIGNAL PROBLEM

"The bottleneck in algorithmic accountability is not detection. It's translation."


You are an independent auditor. You just finished investigating TalentSift, an AI hiring tool used by hundreds of companies.

You found serious problems. Evidence of bias, inflated accuracy claims, no transparency.

Now you have to make your findings matter.


You have 4 approaches to reach 5 possible audiences. You can't reach everyone — choose wisely.

Each audience has different knowledge, different incentives, and different power.

The same evidence, framed differently, produces radically different outcomes.


Your audit is only as good as your ability to translate it.

DEBRIEF

This game demonstrates the translation gap in algorithmic accountability: the distance between having evidence and making it matter.

In reality:

  • Of 435 tools built for algorithmic accountability, only 8 address audit communication
  • The same finding can engage one audience and alienate another
  • Technical evidence rarely moves policymakers. Human stories rarely move engineers.
  • Who you reach first changes who you can reach next
  • While you figure out how to communicate, the system keeps running

Detection is the easy part. Translation is the bottleneck.

The skill this game practices — matching evidence to audience — is the least-supported, most-critical step in the entire accountability pipeline.
