Audit Translator

Turn technical audit findings into communications that move people to act.

1. What did you audit?

Describe the system and the context. You don't need technical precision here — plain language is fine.

2. What did you find?

Describe your findings. Include numbers if you have them, but narrative matters more than metrics here.

3. Who are you communicating to?

Different audiences need different framings. Select your primary audience — you can come back and generate for others.

Policymakers

City council, legislators, regulatory bodies. Need regulatory context, precedent, and specific asks.

Journalists

Reporters covering tech, policy, or local government. Need story angles, human impact, and verifiable claims.

Affected Communities

The people impacted by the system. Need plain language, what it means for them, and what they can do.

Deployer / Company

The organization running the system. Need business risk, liability exposure, and remediation paths.

Regulators

Enforcement agencies, data protection authorities, sector regulators. Need legal frameworks and documented violations.

Funders / Board

Foundations, grant-makers, or board members. Need impact framing, systemic context, and what support enables.

4. Your Communication Brief

Structured guidance and draft language tailored to your audience. Edit and adapt freely.
